The best manifestation app depends on your practice. For daily affirmation reminders, I am is the most popular by far (721,000+ ratings). For gratitude journaling, Gratitude: Self-Care Journal leads. 1. Manifest: Vision Board & 369 — best for the 369 method

Manifest: Vision Board & 369 (by ManifestAI — that's us) is the only app on this list designed around the 369 method as a first-class practice: it stores your affirmation, opens the morning, afternoon, and night sessions at the right windows, counts the 3, 6, and 9 repetitions, and tracks the 33-day cycle with streaks and a streak freeze. Around that core it adds grid-based vision boards, a gratitude journal with optional AI elevation, and a daily numerology reading — so one app covers what would otherwise take three. Honest limitation: it's iPhone-only (no Android or web), it's new — so it has no rating history yet — and after the 3-day free trial the full experience requires a subscription.

2. I am - Daily Affirmations — best for affirmation reminders

I am (Monkey Taps) is the giant of the category: 721,000+ US ratings at 4.8 stars. It does one thing extremely well — delivering affirmations from a huge library as scheduled notifications and beautiful home-screen widgets, so positive prompts reach you all day without opening the app. Premium (monthly, yearly, or lifetime) unlocks the full library. Honest limitation: it's a receive-only experience — there's no journaling, no 369 structure, and no vision board, so it supplements a manifestation practice rather than guiding one.

3. Gratitude: Self-Care Journal — best for gratitude journaling

Gratitude (Hapjoy Technologies) is the best-rated journal-first app in the niche — 4.9 stars across ~45,000 ratings. You get a prompted gratitude journal with photos and voice notes, affirmations you can record in your own voice, vision boards, and a Daily Zen feed. It's polished, private, and genuinely pleasant to use daily. Honest limitation: it's a self-care app more than a manifestation app — there's no 369 method or numerology, and manifestation-specific structure is light, so method-driven practitioners will be assembling their own routine.

4. Stella - Manifest Anything — best for AI audio manifestation

Stella (Perl Productions) takes a different route: instead of writing, you listen. It generates personalized audio narratives that describe your dream life as if it's already happening, tailored to the intentions you enter, and works offline. If visualization is your strongest muscle and writing feels like a chore, this is the standout. Honest limitation: it's audio-first by design — there's no real writing practice, vision board, or 369 structure, and the personalized features sit behind the subscription.

5. Manifest & Affirmations - Soul — best for personalized affirmations

Soul (Matthew Leong) solves the "generic affirmation" problem: you type your specific desire and it generates affirmations crafted for exactly that situation, then delivers them at optimal times with widgets and music-backed visualizations. At 4.9 stars it has the happiest users of any smaller app here. Honest limitation: it's deliberately single-purpose — affirmations are the whole product, premium is annual-only, and there's no journal, board, or method tracking around them.

6. Law of Attraction Toolbox — best for guided LOA exercises

Law of Attraction Toolbox (Inspired Apps) is the practitioner's choice: 25+ guided exercises drawn from Abraham-Hicks, Neville Goddard, and Napoleon Hill — scripting, the Focus Wheel, the 68-second timer, a money game — plus an AI coach and a manifestation goals tracker. If you've read the books and want structure for the techniques, nothing else here goes as deep. Honest limitation: it's built squarely on the Abraham-Hicks school of teaching — if that framework isn't yours, much of the toolbox won't resonate, and the sheer number of tools can feel overwhelming for beginners.

7. Vision Board & Goal Tracker — best for simple vision boards

Vision Board & Goal Tracker (Sovereign Apps) keeps it simple: build an aesthetic vision board from photos and quotes, save it as your wallpaper, and pin it — or custom-designed affirmation cards — to your home screen as widgets. It's the fastest path from "I want a vision board on my phone" to actually having one. Honest limitation: boards and widgets are the whole app — there's no journaling, no guided method, and no daily practice beyond looking at what you've made. (For the DIY route, see our guide to making a vision board on your phone.)

8. Myla: Manifest & Vision Board — best for a calm daily ritual

Myla (Succeed Software) is the closest competitor to an all-in-one: daily intentions, vision boards, affirmations, a guided 369 flow, and Solfeggio frequency audio, wrapped in a deliberately quiet cream-and-gold journal aesthetic. If you want your practice to feel like a slow ritual rather than a productivity app, Myla nails the mood. Honest limitation: it's still building its track record — under 700 ratings so far — and expanded boards, tools, and frequencies sit behind the Premium subscription.

Frequently asked questions

What app do people use for the 369 method?

Manifest: Vision Board & 369 is built specifically around the 369 method — guided sessions, repetition counters, and 33-day cycle tracking — and Myla includes a guided 369 flow inside its broader ritual. Most apps actually named "369" on the App Store are either brand new with no ratings yet or poorly rated (the longest-standing one, 369 Manifestation & Meditation, sits at 2.3 stars). Many people simply use a notes app — which works, but gives you no reminders, counters, or streaks. New to the method? Start with how to do the 369 method.

Are manifestation apps free?

Every app in this comparison is free to download, and all eight monetize the same way: a free tier plus an optional premium subscription. Some (Manifest, Stella) offer a free trial of the full experience; others (I am, Gratitude) let you use a limited free version indefinitely. Fully free manifestation apps with no paid tier are rare on iOS.

What is the most popular manifestation app?

By ratings volume, I am - Daily Affirmations is the most popular manifestation-adjacent app on the App Store — 721,000+ US ratings at 4.8 stars as of July 2026. Gratitude: Self-Care Journal is the most popular journal-first option (~45,000 ratings, 4.9 stars).

Do manifestation apps actually work?

There's no scientific evidence that manifestation itself attracts outcomes. What the better apps genuinely help with are the parts that do have support: writing down specific goals, practicing gratitude regularly, and keeping a daily habit — reminders, streaks, and structure make you far more likely to stay consistent than a blank notebook. Treat any app as a consistency tool, not a guarantee, and pair it with real action (our beginner's guide to manifesting covers that balance).
